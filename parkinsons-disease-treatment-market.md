Transdermal patches deliver medication through the skin, offering a steady release and minimizing gastrointestinal side effects. Subcutaneous methods involve injections that can provide rapid relief, while intestinal infusion delivers drugs directly into the digestive system, ensuring consistent medication levels and reducing fluctuations in symptoms. Each method has distinct advantages tailored to patient needs.</p></p>
